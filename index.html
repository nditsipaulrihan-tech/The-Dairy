About The Book

Before she was your mother, she was a girl with dreams. She wanted to play football. She wanted to give her life to God. She wanted to build something of her own. Instead, she woke at 3 a.m, walked to the sugarcane fields in the dark, brewed corn beer on weekends, stretched every salary envelope beyond its limits, and carried the weight of survival so her children could have a future. She never wrote any of it down. She was too busy living the story. The Diary She Never Wrote is the story her mother never had time to tell-recorded by the daughter she raised to hold the pen. Set in Cameroon and rooted in the realities of many African homes, this book is a moving reflection on sacrifice, discipline, imperfection, and a mother's quiet, enduring love. This is more than one woman's story. It is a tribute to every mother who gave everything she had and asked for nothing in return. Give her flowers while she can still smell them.

About The Author

Kum Doris Mbeuh is a Cameroonian writer, educator, and entrepreneur based in
Yaoundé, Cameroon. Passionate about storytelling and human development.
She writes with honesty, emotional depth, and cultural authenticity.
Her debut book, The Diary She Never Wrote, is a heartfelt tribute to the life, sacrifices,
and enduring wisdom of her mother. Through her writing, Doris seeks to preserve
stories often left untold and inspire people to cherish and
celebrate their loved ones while they still can. Beyond writing, she works in
accounting, data analysis, research, and youth empowerment,
believing that some stories are too important to remain unwritten.
